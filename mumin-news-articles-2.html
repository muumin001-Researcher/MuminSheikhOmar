Regional Leadership

East Africa Leadership Training at Jigjiga Management Institute

26 APR 2026By Mumin Sheikh Omar Ahmed

A profound honor: I was officially invited to a prestigious leadership programme hosted by the College of Management and Public Service in Jigjiga, Ethiopia's Somali Region.

This remarkable initiative brought together emerging leaders from across East Africa, creating an invaluable platform for exchanging ideas on governance, strategic management, and regional integration.

Keynote Address on Collaborative Leadership

As part of this distinguished gathering, I had the privilege of delivering a keynote speech focused on collaborative governance and the role of educational institutions in nurturing the next generation of leaders. The session emphasized how universities like New Generation University can serve as catalysts for regional development by fostering cross-border academic partnerships and knowledge exchange.

The strength of our region lies not in our individual titles, but in our shared commitment to excellence and collaborative progress. When educational institutions join hands across borders, we create an unstoppable force for positive change.

Practical Insights and Development Projects

Beyond the conference sessions, our team visited several development projects across the region, gaining practical insights into ongoing initiatives and their community impact — a rare chance to see how strategic leadership translates into tangible improvements in infrastructure and public service delivery.

Building Regional Networks

The programme facilitated meaningful connections with educators, policymakers, and development practitioners from Ethiopia, Djibouti, Kenya, and Somalia — networks already bearing fruit through ongoing discussions about student exchange and joint research.

I extend my sincere appreciation to Dr. Nimcan Ibrahim and his exceptional team for their outstanding organization and unwavering commitment to nurturing the next generation of leaders.

Entrepreneurship

Guiding NGU Students Through Entrepreneurship Week

18 NOV 2025By Mumin Sheikh Omar Ahmed

I was honored to guide NGU students during International Entrepreneurship Week, as they presented business ideas built on creativity and a real commitment to Somaliland's future.

Over several intensive workshops, I worked closely with students in the Entrepreneurship & Small Business Management course, teaching business plan development, market analysis, financial projections, and pitch presentation — not as an academic exercise, but as training for real ventures.

Diiqo-Bila: A Cultural Innovation

One remarkable idea to emerge was Diiqo-Bila, inspired by the traditional Somali diiqo gifts given after weddings by the bride's family — sharing foods like Muqmaq, cultural dances, and songs celebrating the union of two families.

The student team built a plan to modernize this tradition: renting and preparing cultural dresses, Xeedho, and singers who perform songs honoring both families.

Honored to guide NGU students in Entrepreneurship Week as they presented visionary business ideas like Diiqo-Bila — proof of their creativity and commitment to Somaliland's future.

Preserving Heritage While Innovating

The presentation showed how NGU students preserve cultural heritage while innovating for modern needs — entrepreneurship rooted in local context yet globally competitive. Several plans from this week have since drawn interest from local investors and incubators.

Field Experience

Real-World Learning: NGU Students Explore Berbera

22 NOV 2025By Mumin Sheikh Omar Ahmed

Academic leadership means bridging theory and practice — so I organized a field visit for our Business & Economics students to Berbera, one of the Horn of Africa's most important trade hubs.

Students toured the Berbera Port, the Berbera Free Zone, and Egal Park, seeing firsthand how this historic maritime facility has become a modern logistics and trade hub — with real, guided exposure to projects recently completed on the ground.

Every journey is a lesson learned and an experience gained.

Connecting Classroom Theory to Practice

The visit complemented coursework in Business and Economics, letting students observe trade facilitation, free-zone economics, and infrastructure investment as living examples rather than textbook cases. Students moved through in small shifts, so each group received close, guided attention.

A Shared Institutional Memory

Beyond the technical learning, the trip built camaraderie among students across different shifts — turning a single field visit into a memory the whole cohort now shares, and reflecting NGU's commitment to graduates who understand Somaliland's economy from the ground up.

A Historic Day: Celebrating Somaliland's International Recognition

26 DEC 2025By Mumin Sheikh Omar Ahmed

On this historic day, New Generation University extends its heartfelt congratulations to the people and government of Somaliland on the long-awaited achievement of international recognition.

For decades, the people of Somaliland endured hardship with resilience, upheld peace with dignity, and built a democratic system of governance without external assistance — a commitment to stability and self-determination now rightfully acknowledged by the international community.

A great tribute to our journey! Well done, NGU, for highlighting the importance of this milestone for the future of our students and the entire nation.

New Opportunities for NGU Students

This recognition opens new horizons for collaboration, education, innovation, and global engagement — enhanced international partnerships and research collaborations that were previously difficult to establish.

As an institution dedicated to academic excellence, NGU celebrates this milestone with profound pride, saluting the courage, patience, and determination of the people of Somaliland.
